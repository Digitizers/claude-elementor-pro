# Local business — studio build pack

**When to use:** the catch-all SMB site — plumber, landscaper, accountant, cleaner, contractor — where no sharper vertical fits. Adapt freely.

## Voice & positioning
Credible, friendly, no-nonsense — "real people who do good work nearby." The visitor wants to confirm you do the thing they need, that you serve their area, and that others trust you — then reach out. Plain language, proof over polish. **Primary conversion: get a quote / contact** (header button, phone visible, form at the bottom). This is the most adaptable pack — swap the service names and area and it fits most trades.

## Design system
- **Palette** (maps onto brand-kit tokens — `brand` / darker shade / `accent` / `heading` / `text` / `muted`). Neutral, trustworthy default; recolor per client:
  - primary `#2E5E4E` · primary-dark `#1E4032` · accent `#E0A93B` · dark `#1C2421` · body-text `#39423E` · muted `#828D88`
  - A grounded green + warm gold reads dependable and local without feeling corporate. If the client has brand colors, use those and keep this as the fallback.
- **Typography:** a clear, friendly sans throughout (Inter or Work Sans) — headings 600, body 400. Easy to read, fast to load.
- **Spacing/rhythm:** balanced — section padding 80–100px, boxed ~1200px. Nothing flashy; clarity over decoration.

## Section flow (top → bottom)
1. **Hero** — what you do + who for + **Get a Quote** CTA; phone number in reach.
2. **Services** — card grid of the core offerings, plain descriptions, each linkable.
3. **About** — short story, license/insurance/credentials, a real photo of the owner or crew.
4. **Why us / trust** — guarantees you can actually back, response time, years in business (facts, not fluff).
5. **Testimonials** — local customer quotes, first name + town.
6. **Service area** — towns/zip codes served, or a map; answers "do you come to me?".
7. **Contact** — quote form, phone, email, hours + footer.

## Build notes
- Use the recipes in `references/recipes.md` (Hero, Services grid, Split for About, Testimonials, Contact); bind colors/fonts to the named tokens in `references/brand-kit.md` so a recolor is a token swap, not a rebuild.
- Keep it **adaptable**: this pack is a starting frame. Rename sections to the trade's language (e.g. "Our Projects" for a contractor, "Packages" for a cleaner) and drop any section that doesn't apply rather than padding it.
- Gotcha: only promise what the client can deliver — "24/7", "same-day", "licensed & insured", "satisfaction guaranteed" must be true. Flag any claim you can't verify as the client's to confirm.
